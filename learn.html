Learn about autism and neurodiversity

What is neurodiversity?

Neurodiversity is an umbrella term that includes several Specific Learning differences and developmental or acquired difficulties which impact daily life and work performance.

These conditions can be found around the spectrum of intellectual ability and include:

  • Dyslexia,
  • Developmental co-ordination disorder (DCD), also known as dyspraxia,
  • Dyscalculia Dysgraphia,
  • ADHD,
  • Autistic Spectrum Conditions

They can affect people in different ways and more than one condition can co-occur.

What is autism?

Autism is an umbrella term for long-life developmental conditions that describe varied conditions characterised by difficulty in social skills, communication, repetition, and repetitive behaviour.

However, it is widely understood to be a neurological brain condition. It is described as a spectrum condition due to individual cognitive differences and perceptions of the world, which result from difficulties or differences in identified problems.
