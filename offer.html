What We Offer

Education Provision

DSA NMH - Disabled Student Allowance Non-Medical Help

As a specialist in One-to-One Study Skills and Specialist Mentor, I support both young and mature students on the spectrum in HE/FE, including apprentices from diverse backgrounds at different transitional stages in their education who are claimants of the Disabled Students Allowance.

Specialist Mentor (AS)

As an AS Mentor, I work with students on the spectrum, in receipt of Disabled Students Allowance, on a one-to-one basis. I identify and overcome barriers to learning, and devise and implement strategies to help students navigate university life. Topics discussed during the mentoring session may include:

  • Sensory issues
  • University life
  • Time management
  • Work-life balance
  • Social communication
  • Group Work placement
  • Prioritising and structure
  • Overcoming learning barriers
  • Managing change, stress and anxiety
  • Managing environmental issues and distractions

However, there may be signposting if necessary for situations that demand the use of a trained counsellor, mental health, and advocate.

AS one-to-one Study Skills

As a specialist AS Study Skills tutor in HE, I support students with identifying their needs to promote independent learning by developing their metacognitive skills and managing their workload. I Increase students' motivation by guiding them to develop various academic and transferable skills to maximise their full potential.

Topics discussed during the mentoring session may include:

  • Exam skills
  • Note-taking
  • Referencing
  • Memory learning
  • Critical thinking
  • Reflective writing
  • Reading effectively
  • Presentation skills
  • Assignment planning
  • Conference papers/posters
  • How to structure assignments
  • Using Microsoft PowerPoint
  • Critically evaluating information.

Employment Provision

Career Transitioning

I support individuals who aim to make a career switch. Individuals may be inclined to disengage from work for over a long period and find work transitioning challenging as a result of environmental factors, sensory issuers, coping with changes and adaptability, social and integration issues, and more, which may impact heavily on their wellbeing.

I offer a tailored support coaching programme that meets individuals' needs in enabling a smoother career transition. Excell Resource coaching package supports discovering the unique strengths that help build crucial skills in attaining the career switcher's dream job in the market.

Pre-Employment Coaching

Quite often, many individuals on the spectrum face many barriers to employment and may have negative attitudes due to past experiences. Clients are supported by helping them overcome challenges and gain experience, the right skill sets, attitudes, and qualifications to obtain and sustain suitable employment.

Our support includes access to work coaching. Access to Work is a publicly funded employment support programme, provided by Department for Works & Pensions (DWP). It is intended for people with disabilities to gain sustainable employment, which aims to assist more people with disabilities to start or stay in work.

The grants are directed at paying for the use of assistive equipment, coaching programs, and making reasonable adjustments/ adaptations to the office environment in relation to the equality legislation Act. Eligibility Criteria include Health condition or disability UK Resident, 16+ years of age, and sets 52 Weeks contract in place.

We could provide support in the following areas for career transitioning and coaching programmes:

  • Self-assertion
  • Leadership skills
  • Interview coaching
  • Sensory issues at work
  • Effective learning at work
  • Overcoming low self-esteem
  • Equality and diversity at work
  • Effective teamwork and meetings
  • Communication and presentation skills
  • CV, application and job searching skills
  • Creating effective working relationships
  • Supporting the workplace assessment package
