Specialist Mentor; AS

As an AS Mentor, I work with students on the spectrum on a one -to one basis in receipt of Disabled Students Allowance. to identify and overcome barriers to learning and devise and implement strategies to help students cope generally with university life. Topics during the mentoring session you may want to discuss include any of the following :

  • Sensory issues
  • Time management
  • Social Communication
  • Group work Work placement
  • Prioritising and structure
  • Overcoming learning barriers
  • Managing change or anxiety University life
  • Managing stress and anxiety Work-life balance
  • Dealing with environmental issues and distractions

However, there may be signposting if necessary for situations that demand the use of a trained counsellor, mental health, and advocate.

Employment Provision

Career Transitioning

I provide support to individuals who aim at making a phase of change. Individuals may be inclined to disengage from work for over a long period and find work transitioning challenging as a result of environmental factors, sensory issuers, coping with changes and adaptability, social and integration issues, etc which may impact heavily on their wellbeing.

I offer a tailored support coaching programme that meets individuals' needs in enabling a smoother career transition. Excell Resource coaching package supports you in discovering the unique strengths that help you build crucial skills in attaining your dream job in the market.

Pre-Employment Coaching

Quite often, many individuals on the spectrum condition face many barriers to employment and may have negative attitudes due to past experiences. Clients are supported by helping them overcome challenges and gain experience, the right skill sets, attitudes, and qualifications to obtain and sustain suitable employment. Our support includes: Access to work coaching Access to Work is a publicly funded employment support programme, provided by Department for Works & Pensions (DWP) It is intended for people with disabilities to gain sustainable employment, which aims to assist more people with disabilities to start or stay in work. The grants are directed at paying for the use of assistive equipment, coaching programs, and making reasonable adjustments/adaptations to the office environment in relation to the equality legislation Act. Eligibility Criteria include Health condition or disability UK Resident, 16+ years of age, and sets 52 Weeks contract in place.
